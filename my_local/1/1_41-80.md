#### 59. Suppose you have an 8-bit binary number N. Which of the following operations does not change its 4 bits ? #cse 

a) An exclusive logical sum of N with OFh 
b) A negative logical product of N with OFh 
c) A logical product of N with OFh 
d) A logical sum of N with OFh 


Ans: c
##### Note:
**XOR with 0Fh** (`00001111`):
- XOR flips bits where the mask is `1`.
- Lower 4 bits: `1010 ^ 1111 = 0101` (**changed**).
- Upper 4 bits: `1010 ^ 0000 = 1010` (unchanged).
- **Result**: `10100101` (lower 4 bits modified).
    
b) **Negative Logical Product (NAND) with 0Fh**:
- NAND inverts the result of an AND operation.
- First compute `N AND 0Fh = 00001010`, then invert: `11110101`.
- Lower 4 bits: `0101` (**changed** from `1010`).
- **Result**: `11110101` (lower 4 bits inverted).

c) **Logical Product (AND) with 0Fh** (`00001111`):
- AND keeps bits only where the mask is `1`.
- Lower 4 bits: `1010 & 1111 = 1010` (**unchanged**).
- Upper 4 bits: `1010 & 0000 = 0000` (masked).
- **Result**: `00001010` (lower 4 bits preserved).
    
d) **Logical Sum (OR) with 0Fh** (`00001111`):
- OR sets bits to `1` where the mask is `1`.
- Lower 4 bits: `1010 | 1111 = 1111` (**changed**).
- Upper 4 bits: `1010 | 0000 = 1010` (unchanged).
- **Result**: `10101111` (lower 4 bits overwritten).
