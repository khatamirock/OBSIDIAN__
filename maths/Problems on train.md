### A jogger running at 9 kmph alongside a railway track in 240 metres ahead of the engine of a 120 metres long train running at 45 kmph in the same direction. In how much time will the train pass the jogger?


$$
\begin{align}
Speed \ of \ train \ relative  \ to \ jogger = (45 - 9) \, \text{km/hr} = 36 \, \text{km/hr}\\
\left(36 \times \frac{5}{18}\right) \, \text{m/sec} \\
&= 10 \, \text{m/sec}.\\
Distance to be covered = (240 + 120) \, \text{m} = 360 \, \text{m}\\
\text{Time taken} &= \frac{360}{10} \, \text{sec} = 36 \, \text{sec}.
\end{align}
$$

### 64. A train went 300 km from city X to city Y at an average speed of 100 km/h. At what speed did it travel on the way back if its average speed for the whole trip was 120 km/h.

To determine the speed at which the train traveled on the return journey from city Y to city X, given that its average speed for the entire trip was 120 km/h, we can follow these steps:

1. **Calculate the total distance of the round trip:**
    
    - The distance from city X to city Y is 300 km
    - Therefore, the total distance for the round trip is
        - 300 km (X to Y) + 300 km (Y to X) = 600 km
2. **Determine the total time taken for the round trip:**
    
    - The average speed for the entire trip is given as 120 km/h.
    - Using the formula:
        - Total Time = Total Distance / Average Speed
        - Total Time = 600 km / 120 km/h = 5 hours.
3. **Calculate the time taken for the journey from city X to city Y:**
    
    - The speed from X to Y is 100 km/h.
    - Using the formula:
        - Time = Distance / Speed
        - Time = 300 km / 100 km/h = 3 hours.
4. **Determine the time taken for the return journey from city Y to city X:**
    
    - Total time for the round trip is 5 hours.
    - Time taken from X to Y is 3 hours.
    - Therefore, time for the return journey (Y to X) is:
        - 5 hours (total) - 3 hours (X to Y) = 2 hours.
5. **Calculate the speed for the return journey:**
    
    - Using the formula:
        - Speed = Distance / Time
        - Speed = 300 km / 2 hours = 150 km/h.

Thus, the train traveled at a speed of **150 km/h** on its return journey from city Y to city X.
