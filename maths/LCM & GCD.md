  
###  The H.C.F. of two numbers is 23 and the other two factors of their L.C.M. are 13 and 14. The larger of the two numbers is:
#### detailed explanation:
 To solve this problem, let's break it down step-by-step and explain the reasoning behind each step.

#### Given:

- HCF (Greatest Common Divisor) of the two numbers = 23
- The other two factors of their LCM are 13 and 14.
- We need to find the larger of the two numbers.
To find the larger of two numbers given that their Highest Common Factor (H.C.F.) is 23 and the other two factors of their Least Common Multiple (L.C.M.) are 13 and 14, let’s break this down step-by-step.

#### Step 1: Understand the Definitions
- **H.C.F. (Highest Common Factor)**: The largest number that divides both numbers without a remainder. Here, the H.C.F. is 23, so both numbers are multiples of 23.
- **L.C.M. (Least Common Multiple)**: The smallest number that is a multiple of both numbers. The problem states that the L.C.M. includes the factor 23 (from the H.C.F.) and has 13 and 14 as "the other two factors."

Since the H.C.F. is 23, we can express the two numbers as:
- Number 1 = 23 × a
- Number 2 = 23 × b
where (a) and (b) are positive integers. Because 23 is the H.C.F., (a) and (b) must be coprime (i.e., their H.C.F. is 1), otherwise the H.C.F. of the two numbers would be a multiple of 23 greater than 23.

#### Step 2: Relate H.C.F. and L.C.M.
For any two numbers (x = 23a) and (y = 23b), where (a) and (b) are coprime: (23 are common between them)
- $H.C.F.((x, y)) = 23$
- $L.C.M.((x, y)) = 23 × a × b$

This follows from the property that:
$\text{L.C.M.}(x, y) \times \text{H.C.F.}(x, y) = x \times y$ 
$Substituting (x = 23a) and (y = 23b):$
$\text{L.C.M.} \times 23 = (23a) \times (23b) = 23^2 \times a \times b$
$\text{L.C.M.} = 23 \times a \times b$
Since (a) and (b) are coprime, their L.C.M. is $(a \times b)$, and the factor 23 comes from the common part.

#### Step 3: Interpret "The Other Two Factors"
The problem states that "the other two factors of their L.C.M. are 13 and 14." Since the L.C.M. is 23 × a × b and includes the factor 23 (from the H.C.F.), the phrase "the other two factors" suggests that after accounting for 23, the remaining part of the L.C.M. involves the factors 13 and 14. The simplest interpretation is that:
- (a = 13) and (b = 14), or
- (a = 14) and (b = 13)

These values imply that the L.C.M. is:
$\text{L.C.M.} = 23 \times 13 \times 14$
The factors of the L.C.M. would then be 23, 13, and 14 (noting that 14 = 2 × 7, but the problem specifies 13 and 14 as factors, not necessarily prime factors). The "other two factors" beyond 23 are indeed 13 and 14, which fits the problem’s wording.

#### Step 4: Check Coprimality
Since (a) and (b) must be coprime:
- 13 is a prime number.
- 14 = 2 × 7, with no factors in common with 13.
- H.C.F.(13, 14) = 1, so 13 and 14 are coprime.

This satisfies the condition that (a) and (b) have no common factors other than 1.

#### Step 5: Calculate the Numbers
The two numbers are:
- $(23 \times 13 = 299) (since (20 \times 13 = 260), (3 \times 13 = 39), (260 + 39 = 299))$
- $(23 \times 14 = 322) (since (20 \times 14 = 280), (3 \times 14 = 42), (280 + 42 = 322))$

So, the numbers are 299 and 322.

#### Step 6: Verify the Conditions
- **H.C.F.**: Factorize the numbers:
  - 299 = 23 × 13
  - 322 = 23 × 14 = 23 × 2 × 7
  - Common factor: 23
  - H.C.F.(299, 322) = 23, which matches.
- **L.C.M.**:
  - L.C.M.(299, 322) = 23 × 13 × 14
  - Compute: $(13 \times 14 = 182) (since (10 \times 14 = 140), (3 \times 14 = 42), (140 + 42 = 182))$
  - Then, $(23 \times 182 = 4186) (since (20 \times 182 = 3640), (3 \times 182 = 546), (3640 + 546 = 4186))$
  - L.C.M. = 4186
  - Factors include 23, 13, and 14 (since 4186 = 23 × 13 × 14), and "the other two factors" after 23 are 13 and 14, which holds.

#### Step 7: Consider Alternatives
Could (a) or (b) include additional factors? For example, (a = 13 \times k) and (b = 14), but (k) must be coprime with 14, and this would increase the L.C.M. beyond just 23 × 13 × 14, introducing more factors, contradicting the "two factors" condition. The simplest numbers that satisfy all conditions are with (a = 13) and (b = 14) (or vice versa).

#### Step 8: Identify the Larger Number
- 299 < 322
- The larger number is 322.

Thus, the larger of the two numbers is 322.
