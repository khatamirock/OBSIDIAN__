### 63. A floor with dimension of 20 feet to 35 feet is needed to be tiled. Two workers can tile that floor in `2 hours and 30 minutes`. If they are joined by three other workers of similar ability. How many hours will it take to tile the floor?

To solve this, let's break it down step by step:

---

#### Given:

1. **Floor dimensions**: 20 ft × 35 ft (not needed for calculation since time and worker ability are provided).
2. **Two workers' time**: 2 hours and 30 minutes = **2.5 hours**.
3. **Additional workers**: Three more workers join, making a total of **5 workers**.
4. **Assumption**: All workers have the same ability and efficiency.

---

#### Step 1: Calculate the total work done.

- ==**Work** is measured in "worker-hours."==  ==**main IDEA**==
	- given ==2 workers== and ==Hours== -> 2.5 hrs
- If **2 workers** can complete the tiling in **2.5 hours**, the ==total work is:==
    
    **Total work** = ==Number of workers × Time taken==  
    **Total work** = 2 × 2.5 = **5 worker-hours**.
    

---

#### Step 2: Calculate the time for 5 workers.

- Now, with **5 workers** doing the same total work, the time required is:
    
    **Time required** = Total work ÷ Number of workers  
    **Time required** = 5 ÷ 5 = **1 hour**.
    

---

#### Final Answer:

It will take **1 hour** for 5 workers to tile the floor.

---

#### Similar Examples:

1. **Example 1**:  
    If 3 workers take 6 hours to paint a wall, how long will it take 6 workers of equal efficiency?
    
    - **Work** = 3 × 6 = **18 worker-hours**.
    - With 6 workers: **18 ÷ 6 = 3 hours**.
2. **Example 2**:  
    If 4 workers can mow a lawn in 4 hours, how long will 8 workers take?
    
    - **Work** = 4 × 4 = **16 worker-hours**.
    - With 8 workers: **16 ÷ 8 = 2 hours**.






### To determine how many days it will take for 5 people to complete a full job, given that 10 people can do half the job in 10 days

Here’s the formatted text:

---

#### Work Problem: Determining Days for 5 People

To determine how many days it will take for 5 people to complete a full job, given that 10 people can do half the job in 10 days, let’s solve this step by step.

#### Step 1: Understand the Work Done by 10 People
We’re told that 10 people can complete half the job in 10 days. To find a useful measure, let’s calculate the total ==effort/rate== in terms of “person-days” for this task:

- **Number of people**: 10  
- **Days worked**: 10  
- **Work completed**: Half the job ($\frac{1}{2}$)

The total effort is:

$$
10 \text{ people} \times 10 \text{ days} = 100 \text{ person-days}
$$

So, 100 person-days are required to complete half the job.

#### Step 2: Calculate Person-Days for the Full Job
Since 100 person-days correspond to half the job, the full job, being twice as much work, requires twice the effort:

$$
100 \text{ person-days} \times 2 = 200 \text{ person-days}
$$

Thus, the full job requires 200 person-days.

#### Step 3: Determine Days Needed for 5 People
Now, we need to find out how many days it takes for 5 people to accumulate 200 person-days. Let’s denote the number of days as $ D $. The total person-days when 5 people work for $ D $ days is:

$$
5 \text{ people} \times D \text{ days} = 5D \text{ person-days}
$$

This must equal the total effort required for the full job:

$$
5D = 200
$$

Solving for $ D $:

$$
D = \frac{200}{5} = 40
$$

So, it takes 5 people **40 days** to complete the full job.

#### ==Verification with Work Rate==
Let’s confirm this using the work rate approach. First, find the rate at which 10 people work:

- **Work done**: $\frac{1}{2}$ job  
- **Time**: 10 days  
- **Rate of 10 people**: 

$\frac{\frac{1}{2}}{10} = \frac{1}{20} \text{ job per day}$

If 10 people together do $\frac{1}{20}$ of the job per day, ==one person’s rate **is==**:

$\frac{\frac{1}{20}}{10} = \frac{1}{200} \text{ job per day}$

For 5 people, their combined rate is:

$5 \times \frac{1}{200} = \frac{5}{200} = \frac{1}{40} \text{ job per day}$

To complete the full job (1 job) at this rate:

$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Work}}{\text{Rate}} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{40}} = 40 \text{ days}$

This matches our earlier result.

#### Alternative Check: Proportional Reasoning
If 10 people take 10 days to do half the job, they’d take 20 days to do the full job (since the full job is twice the work). With 5 people—half the workforce—it should take twice as long as 10 people would take for the full job:

$$
20 \text{ days} \times 2 = 40 \text{ days}
$$

This consistency across methods confirms our solution.

####   Final Answer
It will take **5 people 40 days** to complete the full job.
