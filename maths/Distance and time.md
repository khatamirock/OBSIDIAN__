
### 1.   In covering a distance of 30 km, Abhay takes 2 hours more than Sameer. If Abhay doubles his speed, then he would take 1 hour less than Sameer. Abhay's speed is:
#### $sol^n$
Let me help you solve this step by step.

1) Let's say Abhay's speed is 'x' $\frac{km}{hr}$
   And let's say Sameer's speed is 'y' km/hr

2) Given that they both cover 30 km
   * For Abhay: Time = Distance/Speed = 30/x
   * For Sameer: Time = Distance/Speed = 30/y

3) From first condition: ^45917d
   * ==Abhay takes 2 hours more than Sameer==
   * 30/x = 30/y + 2
   * 30/x - 30/y = 2 ...(1)

4) From second condition:
   * If Abhay doubles his speed (2x), he takes 1 hour less than Sameer
   * 30/(2x) = 30/y - 1
   * 30/(2x) - 30/y = -1 ...(2)

5) From equation (1):
   * 30/x - 30/y = 2
   * (30y - 30x)/xy = 2
   * 30(y - x) = 2xy ...(3)

6) From equation (2):
   * 30/(2x) - 30/y = -1
   * (15y - 30x)/xy = -1
   * 15y - 30x = -xy ...(4)

7) From equation (4):
   * 15y - 30x = -xy
   * 15y + xy = 30x
   * y(15 + x) = 30x
   * y = 30x/(15 + x) ...(5)

8) Put this value of y in equation (3):
   * 30(30x/(15 + x) - x) = 2x(30x/(15 + x))
   * 30(30x - x(15 + x))/15 + x = 60x²/(15 + x)
   * 30(30x - 15x - x²) = 60x²
   * 30(15x - x²) = 60x²
   * 450x - 30x² = 60x²
   * 450x = 90x²
   * 5 = x

Therefore, Abhay's speed is 5 km/hr.
