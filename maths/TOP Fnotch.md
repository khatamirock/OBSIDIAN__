#### Q1. A man rowing a boat at an speed of five kilometer per hour if the speed of the current is one kilometer per hour and the man is so gone to a place and came back and this round trip needed one hour, how far is the place ?
##### ans:
Let $s_r = 5$ be the rowing speed
Let $s_c = 1$ be the current speed
Let $d$ be the distance
Let $t = 1$ be the total time

Going downstream:
$s_{down} = s_r + s_c = 6$
$t_{down} = \frac{d}{s_{down}} = \frac{d}{6}$

Going upstream:
$s_{up} = s_r - s_c = 4$
$t_{up} = \frac{d}{s_{up}} = \frac{d}{4}$

Total time equation:
$t_{down} + t_{up} = t$
$\frac{d}{6} + \frac{d}{4} = 1$

Finding common denominator:
$\frac{2d}{12} + \frac{3d}{12} = 1$
$\frac{5d}{12} = 1$

Solving for d:
$5d = 12$
$d = \frac{12}{5} = 2.4$

Therefore, $d = 2.4$ kilometers



#### Q2.   
the average of 7 number is 40 and we add another three number which has average of 21 so what would be the total average of these 10 numbers ?
##### Ans:
Let me help you solve this step by step to understand how averages combine.

For the first 7 numbers:
Average = $\frac{\text{Sum of numbers}}{\text{Count of numbers}}$
$40 = \frac{\text{Sum}_1}{7}$
$\text{Sum}_1 = 40 \times 7 = 280$

For the next 3 numbers:
$21 = \frac{\text{Sum}_2}{3}$
$\text{Sum}_2 = 21 \times 3 = 63$

For all 10 numbers:
The total sum = $\text{Sum}_1 + \text{Sum}_2 = 280 + 63 = 343$
The new average = $\frac{\text{Total sum}}{\text{Total count}} = \frac{343}{10} = 34.3$

Therefore, when we combine all 10 numbers, their average is 34.3.

This demonstrates an ==important principle==: when combining groups with different averages, we need to consider ==both the averages and the number of items in each group==. The final average will be weighted toward the larger group's average.
