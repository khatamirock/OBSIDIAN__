### **Types of Security Attacks**  

#### **1. Snooping**  
- **Definition**: ==Unauthorized viewing== or interception of data.  
- **Example**: Eavesdropping on network traffic to steal passwords.  
- **Incorrect Here**: The student didn’t intercept data; they physically broke into the office.  

#### **2. Repudiation**  
- **Definition**: ==Denial of involvement== in an action (e.g., ==denying a transaction==).  
- **Example**: A user denies sending a malicious email.  
- **Incorrect Here**: The student isn’t denying their actions; they’re actively breaking in.  

#### **3. Masquerading**  
- **Definition**: Pretending to be someone else to gain unauthorized access.  
- **Example**: Using stolen credentials to log into a system.  
- **Correct Here**: The student is impersonating someone with legitimate access to the exam (e.g., a professor or staff member).  

#### **4. Replaying**  
- **Definition**: Capturing and retransmitting data to exploit a system.  
- **Example**: Reusing a captured authentication token to gain access.  
- **Incorrect Here**: The student isn’t replaying data; they’re physically breaking in.

#### 🔒 **1. Confidentiality**

👉  
**Confidentiality ensures that information is accessible ==only to authorized users== and protected from unauthorized access.**

### 📌 Example

- Password protection
    
- Encryption
    

---

#### 🖊️ **2. Non-Repudiation**

👉  
**Non-repudiation ensures that a sender cannot deny sending a message or performing an action.**

### 📌 Example

- Digital signatures
    

---

#### ✅ **3. Authenticity**

👉  
**Authenticity ensures that the user, message, or device is genuine and verified.**

### 📌 Example

- Login authentication
    
- Certificates
    

---

#### 🛡️ **4. Integrity**

👉  
**Integrity ensures that data is ==not altered== or modified during transmission or storage.**

### 📌 Example

- Hashing
    
- Checksums
    

---

#### 🌐 **5. Availability**

👉  
**Availability ensures that systems, services, and data are ==accessible== to authorized users whenever needed.**

### 📌 Example

- Backup systems
    
- Protection against DoS attacks
    

---

##### ⚡ One-line summary (exam gold)

👉  
**“Confidentiality protects secrecy, integrity protects correctness, availability ensures access, authenticity verifies identity, and non-repudiation prevents denial of actions.”**

---

#### 🔺 CIA Triad

- Confidentiality
    
- Integrity
    
- Availability
    

The other two are additional security services.


---

#### **Other Security Attacks**  
1. **Tampering**: Unauthorized modification of data.  
   - Example: Altering exam questions after breaking into the office.  
2. **Denial of Service (DoS)**: Overloading a system to make it unavailable.  
   - Example: Flooding a server with requests to crash it.  
3. **Phishing**: Tricking users into revealing sensitive information.  
   - Example: Sending fake emails to steal login credentials.
