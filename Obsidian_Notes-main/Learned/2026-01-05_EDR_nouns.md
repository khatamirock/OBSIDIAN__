## 1. Understand the Business Environment (Before Thinking About Tables)

The company is an **online merchandise seller**. From the description, four major business activities jump out:

1. **Customers browse and place orders**
    
2. **Orders are fulfilled and shipped**
    
3. **Inventory is tracked**
    
4. **Suppliers are used to reorder items when stock is low**
    

👉 These activities naturally lead us to **entities** (things the business cares about and stores data on).

---

## 2. Identify Core Entities (High-Level Thinking)

Ask a simple question:

> “What are the main _nouns_ in this business?”

From the description, reasonable first-pass entities are:

1. **Customer** – who buys items
    
2. **Order** – a purchase transaction
    
3. **Advertised_Item** – products sold on the website
    
4. **Supplier** – companies that provide items
    
5. **Purchased_Item (Order Line Item)** – which items appear on which orders
    

That gives us **5 entities**, which is exactly what the exercise expects.

⚠️ **At this stage**, we are **not worrying about mistakes**. Some errors are intentional and will be fixed during ==normalization==.

## 4️⃣ Why This Matters Before Normalization

Normalization depends on:
- Correct identification of keys
- Correct identification of relationships

Bad naming:

- Hides relationships
- Creates false duplicates
- Makes the data model look correct when it’s actually wrong
    

👉 **Naming cleanup comes before normalization**, not after.

# Mutually Exclusive Data

### What does “mutually exclusive” mean?
It means:
> Two attributes **cannot both be true at the same time** for one entity.

## Key rule to **==remember==**
> **If attributes represent choices, convert them into values of a single attribute.**

```
Employee
Married  ❌
Single   ❌
```

```
Employee
MaritalStatus  ✅
```

## 1️⃣ Clearer attribute meanings
Example:
- `ClothingFlag` + `HealthFlag` ❌  
    ➡ replaced by    
- `ItemDepartment` ✅

Why?
- Flags are mutually exclusive
- Items belong to one department
- **==Departments can expand in the future==**

Example values:
- `M` = Married
- `S` = Single
📌 This is cleaner, extensible, and normalized.


## FOR NORMALIZATION:

WE should fix and correct:

- Attribute names are consistent (no synonyms)
- Attribute meanings are clear (no homonyms)
- ==Redundant and mutually exclusive data are fixed==
👉 **You are finally ready to normalize.**


## Important Principle

> **Logical design first, physical design later**

- Logical design = entities, attributes, relationships
    
- Physical design = indexes, partitions, denormalization
    

You are still in the **logical design phase**.
