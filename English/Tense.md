### **When to Use "For" and "Since"**

- **"For"**: Refers to a duration of time.
    
    - Example: _I have been working here **for five years.**_
    - Use "for" when you know the **length** of time (e.g., hours, days, years).
- **"Since"**: Refers to a point in time.
    
    - Example: _I have been working here **since 2018.**_
    - Use "since" when referring to the **starting point** of an action.

---

### **Difference Between "Have/Has Been" and "Have/Has"**

1. **"Have/Has"**:
    
    - Used with the **present perfect tense** to describe actions:
        - That started in the past and are complete.
        - That have an impact on the present.
    - Examples:
        - _I **have finished** my homework._ (Action is complete.)
        - _He **has visited** Paris three times._ (Focus on the result or frequency.)
2. **"Have/Has Been"**:
    
    - Used with the **present perfect continuous tense** to describe actions:
        - That started in the past and are still happening.
        - That emphasize the duration of the action.
    - Examples:
        - _I **have been reading** this book for hours._ (Action is ongoing.)
        - _She **has been working** here since morning._ (Duration emphasized.)

---

### **Broad Explanation of "Have Been" vs. "Has Been"**

- Use **"have been"** with plural subjects or "I/You/We/They":
    
    - _We **have been traveling** for weeks._
    - _I **have been studying** for the exam._
- Use **"has been"** with singular third-person subjects (He/She/It):
    
    - _He **has been sleeping** since noon._
    - _The machine **has been running** smoothly._

---

### **Common Scenarios**

#### 1. Present Perfect Continuous (With Duration):

- **"Have been/Has been" + Verb (ing)** is used to show ongoing actions:
    - _I **have been working** here for 5 years._
    - _She **has been waiting** for an hour._

#### 2. Present Perfect (With Result or Experience):

- **"Have/Has" + Past Participle** is used to emphasize completion or frequency:
    - _I **have completed** my tasks._
    - _She **has visited** London three times._

---

### **Additional Examples to Illustrate the Differences**

#### Without "For" or "Since":

- _He **has been sick**_ (state continues into the present).
- _She **has improved**_ (focus on the result of improvement).

#### With "For" or "Since":

- _They **have been living** here **for 10 years.**_
- _We **have been waiting** here **since 8 a.m.**_

---

### **Key Tips to Remember**

1. Use **"have/has been" + verb(-ing)** to emphasize **duration** or **continuity**.
2. Use **"for"** to express the **length** of time and **"since"** to express the **starting point**.
3. Use **"have/has" + past participle** for **completed actions** or experiences.
